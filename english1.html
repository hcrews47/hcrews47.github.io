I took a Literature of the South class my first semester of college that was incredible. I wanted more like this.

Senior year of high school was when I really began to enjoy reading. I realized that books were more than just plot and characters, it was art. I was hooked.

The two primary aspects of a good novel are the writing style and the novel's implications. Plot is secondary.

Last summer, I couldn't stop reading. I decided I'd minor in English.

Many of these books were introduced to me in a class, others I found myself.

My favorite authors tend to be from the modern era. I love the revolutionary styles of authors like Woolf, Faulkner, Vonnegut, and Nabokov.

For most of high school, I wanted to read and write as little as possible. I applied to engineering schools and STEM programs in order to evade English classes. However, by the time I graduated, I realized that I would miss the humanities side of education.

I like Dostoevsky and Nietzsche for their philosophical writing.
